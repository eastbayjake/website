About

I enjoy creating rich, dynamic web applications that fully utilize the power of modern browsers. I have full-stack experience but I'm most passionate about front-end engineering. I'm equally comfortable writing SQL queries or getting out of the office and interacting with customers about product features.

My favorite project was contract work for Wedgies, a startup trying to make online polling less painful. I loved seeing my commits immediately deploy for major clients like USA Today!

My Story

I started programming in 1997 by modifying files in SimCity and Civilization to alter game units and change AI behavior. I picked up HTML/CSS in middle school when I got my own domain name, then learned some Java in a high school programming class.

I studied history at Yale but kept gravitating towards technology. I interned for two years at a marketing firm that specializes in web design for business clients, apprenticing with designers and web developers on projects for Epson and Inspiration Software.

After graduation I joined Teach For America and taught for two years in the Mississippi Delta. I then worked as a lobbyist on education policy. In each of those roles, I kept trying to utilize technology to cut down on repetitive tasks — automating student diagnostics, creating a website for parents, and managing e-mail design and deployment for a statewide campaign about public charter schools. I took online courses and tinkered with personal projects to expand my skill set.

I finally decided to work full-time as a software engineer. After helping pass historic charter school legislation in Mississippi, I joined Hack Reactor to formalize and extend over a decade of self-taught programming knowledge.
